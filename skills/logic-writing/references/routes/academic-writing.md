# Academic-writing route

The academic route creates or substantively revises the final scholarly
artifact. It owns planning, evidence integration, prose, postwrite review, and
final closure.

## 1. Define the artifact and revision contract

Record audience, genre, research question, contribution, requested units,
preservation constraints, citation style, file format, and final deliverable.
For revision, distinguish added, rewritten, moved, omitted, and unresolved
material; never silently drop source content.

Build a source-unit manifest before changing a supplied artifact. Give every
chapter, section, paragraph block, figure, table, footnote group, appendix, and
other material unit a stable identity and locator. Record whether it must be
preserved, may be rewritten, may move, or needs an explicit user decision. The
default is preservation: absence from the new draft is not evidence that an old
unit was intentionally removed.

## 2. Model the real structure

Use LogicGuard structured-artifact review on actual chapters, sections,
paragraphs, figures, and tables. Every important unit needs a parent
contribution, a downstream consumer or explicit terminal treatment, and a
clear relation to adjacent same-level units.

Literature review units should extend, narrow, contrast, depend on, or expose a
limit in what came before. If they are intentionally parallel background, say
so. Local fluency does not rescue a structurally unused section.

For each important unit, keep a structural-contribution row with:

- the question or parent contribution it serves;
- the contribution type, such as definition, evidence, mechanism, contrast,
  method choice, limitation, synthesis, or implication;
- its relation to same-level neighbors;
- the later unit that consumes it, or an explicit terminal disposition;
- the evidence or model identity on which the contribution depends;
- the current gap, repair, or removal decision.

A section interface states what the reader already knows on entry, what the
section must establish, what it is allowed to assume, and what the next section
will receive. This prevents individually fluent sections from becoming an
unconnected collection.

## 3. Deepen important shallow units

Use LogicGuard model deepening before final prose when a high-importance unit
is only a label or list. A method unit normally needs the design need, selected
choice, rejected alternative, implementation consequence, validation
consequence, and limitation. Missing evidence becomes a bounded gap, not
invented detail.

Apply two specific depth gates where relevant:

- A literature progression gate checks whether each source group extends,
  narrows, contrasts, depends on, or limits prior work. A catalogue of summaries
  is not progression.
- A method-depth gate checks the design need, chosen approach, rejected
  alternative, implementation consequence, validation consequence, applicable
  conditions, and limitation. A named method without this chain is still
  shallow.

LogicGuard artifact synthesis may provide story plans and paragraph blueprints.
Those are plans, not final prose and not postwrite evidence.

For an important paragraph, the blueprint should identify its job, incoming
idea, new claim, reason or warrant, evidence, necessary qualification, and
outgoing handoff. Use it as a writing aid, then judge the actual paragraph; do
not paste the blueprint labels into the document.

## 4. Fill evidence gaps without transferring ownership

Send a bounded investigation request with `gap_id`, target claim/unit, needed
source roles, time/scope boundary, and return contract. Verify the returned
packet fingerprint. If it is partial or unavailable, downgrade, omit, or mark
the affected claim for review.

## 5. Write and integrate

Write reader-native academic prose from a ReaderBrief. Explain concepts before
using them, connect each paragraph's old information to its new information,
and integrate citations, footnotes, figures, and tables into the reasoning.
Do not paste route labels, ledgers, blueprints, or internal status prose into
the artifact.

For DOCX/Word/Google Docs mutation, tracked changes, comments, styles, render,
and page inspection, use Documents. For PDF extraction, creation, rendering,
and visual inspection, use PDF. Keep semantic, textual, and layout receipts
separate.

Citations must sit on the claims they support and use a source whose role fits
the claim. Announcements may establish stated intent but not completed
execution; method descriptions may establish design but not outcome; secondary
summaries may orient the reader but do not silently replace an available
primary source. Important footnotes must add a real qualification, source, or
technical detail rather than becoming a hiding place for core reasoning.

Every figure and table needs a reasoning job, an in-text introduction, a clear
caption, interpretable units and labels, a source or derivation, and a later
sentence explaining what the reader should conclude. Visual presence alone is
not analytical contribution.

## 6. Audit the actual artifact

Rebuild the structural map and reverse outline from the current final file or
text. Check source-role fit, progression, method depth, provenance, paragraph
handoffs, citations, figures/tables, limitations, and genre. Any material edit
stales affected receipts. Only the academic route may close the final artifact.

Reconcile the final revision ledger against the original source-unit manifest:
each original unit must be preserved, rewritten, moved, intentionally omitted
with a reason, or visibly unresolved with a next owner. Each new unit must have
a target locator and reason. For tracked-change or comment deliverables, verify
the real file contains the requested markings; a prose summary of changes is
not a document-mutation receipt.

Review paragraph pairs and section boundaries in context, not only isolated
sentences. Repair modes include clarifying a referent, moving an explanation
before first use, adding a missing warrant or limitation, merging repetition,
splitting an overloaded unit, and rewriting the handoff to state the real
relationship. Avoid cosmetic transition words when the logical relation itself
is missing.

Apply the shared writing contract to important sections and paragraphs. Record
the incoming reader state, new contribution, changed state, unresolved item,
and exact downstream consumer. At major handoffs, recover the research
question and explain how the contribution advances it; “this leads to the next
section” is not a handoff.

Treat explanation pressure as a repair signal. Replace prose that announces
what a section intends to prove with the evidence, reasoning, definition, or
comparison that actually does the work. Give technical, institutional, quoted,
and author-summary language an explicit register owner, and introduce terms
before relying on them. Repetition is permitted only when it creates a changed
effect such as escalation, contrast, narrower scope, stronger evidence, or a
new implication.

Do not hide execution friction in a smooth synthesis: access limits, failed
methods, missing data, non-comparable measures, document constraints, and
unresolved review decisions stay near the conclusions they change. Bind every
important structural/model row to exact current artifact spans and reject
unbound prose, unrealized rows, or bindings to an older draft.
