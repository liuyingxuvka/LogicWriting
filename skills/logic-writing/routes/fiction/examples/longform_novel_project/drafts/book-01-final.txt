Chapter 1

Mira found the archive key in the wrong drawer. The numbered seal was intact, but the brass teeth carried fresh graphite. She closed her hand around it and said nothing while the morning clerk signed the ordinary ledger.

Chapter 2

Three access records contradicted one another. Mira copied each time into the margin of her own notebook, then left the official page untouched. The contradiction was more useful while nobody knew she had seen it.

Chapter 3

At the public desk, Mira asked for the map register in front of six witnesses. The request protected the record and exposed her purpose. When the clerk refused, everyone in the room learned which question mattered.

Chapter 4

The rival's ledger named the map room twice, too neatly. Mira followed the second entry to a glass case in the west gallery and understood that the obvious destination had been bait.

Chapter 5

Mira secured the map before the council arrived. She accepted the inquiry that would follow and left the question of the map's origin open. Control had changed hands; the cost was now public.
