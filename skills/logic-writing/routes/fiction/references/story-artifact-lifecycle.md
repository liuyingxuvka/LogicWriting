# Story Artifact Lifecycle

Use this reference for every substantive StorylineDesign task: premise
development, story audit, outline repair, scene planning, short story drafting,
chapter batch work, novel repair, book planning, or final prose closure.

The lifecycle is artifact-size flexible. The user does not need to choose a
route. Infer the requested artifact and then choose evidence depth from
`guard-depth-policy.md`.

## Core Rule

Every story task uses the same Guarded Story Lifecycle. Artifact size changes
the amount of evidence, not whether Guard stages exist. A micro story may use
compact rows. A novel uses layered rows. A short artifact does not get to skip
FlowGuard, TraceGuard, WorldGuard, LogicGuard, SourceGuard, model evidence, or
closure boundaries when those surfaces are material.

## Universal Lifecycle

Run substantive work through this order:

```text
goal intake
-> artifact route and claim-boundary inference
-> FlowGuard process route / story development flowgraph
-> evidence-depth decision
-> StorylineDesign ledger or compact story model
-> child Guard consideration: TraceGuard, WorldGuard, LogicGuard, SourceGuard
-> structure and event movement model
-> scene/chapter contract or compact prose blueprint
-> promise/payoff and continuity check
-> reader-native brief or outline
-> drafting or repair
-> reverse outline or compact postwrite observation
-> closure aggregation
-> delivery with residual gaps
```

## Child Guard Ownership

- FlowGuard owns stage order, child Guard handoff order, evidence freshness,
  stale evidence, skipped checks, artifact versions, and final claim boundary.
- TraceGuard owns true event order, causal chain, protagonist investigation
  order, reader revelation order, trace gaps, and competing explanations.
- WorldGuard owns story-world event, capability, access, resource, authority,
  norm, causal, conflict, and rule consistency claims.
- LogicGuard owns theme, ending interpretation, moral, philosophical, or value
  claims when those claims affect closure.
- SourceGuard owns user material, canon, adaptation, historical facts,
  external sources, and prior-draft support.
- StorylineDesign owns fiction structure, scene/chapter movement, character and
  relationship arcs, promises, prose projection, reverse outlines, and final
  story closure.

## Not Applicable Is Still Evidence

When a child Guard is not relevant, record the surface as
`not_applicable_with_reason`. Do not silently omit it. A good reason names the
claim boundary:

- TraceGuard not applicable because no event order, causality, mystery, hidden
  timeline, or revelation order is being claimed beyond a single static scene.
- WorldGuard not applicable because no material world event, ability, access,
  resource, rule, or consequence supports the requested claim.
- LogicGuard not applicable because the artifact does not assert a material
  theme, ending interpretation, moral, philosophical, or value claim.
- SourceGuard not applicable because no user-supplied canon, prior draft,
  adaptation, historical fact, or external source constrains the output.

## Closure Rule

Before any complete, final, high-quality, publishable, book-level, or
ready-to-use claim, aggregate:

- the FlowGuard process route and evidence-freshness status;
- the StorylineDesign model surface at the selected depth;
- TraceGuard, WorldGuard, LogicGuard, and SourceGuard statuses or
  not-applicable reasons;
- prose projection and reverse-outline or postwrite evidence when prose exists;
- skipped, stale, blocked, unsupported, downgraded, and human-review rows.

The final claim strength is limited by the weakest important current surface.
