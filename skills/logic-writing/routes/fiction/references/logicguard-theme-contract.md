# LogicGuard Theme Contract

Use this reference when a story makes a material theme, ending interpretation,
moral, philosophical, symbolic, or value claim. LogicGuard checks whether the
meaning claimed by the story is structurally supported. It does not decide
whether the prose is beautiful.

## Triggers

Use LogicGuard theme support when:

- the ending states or strongly implies what the story means;
- a character arc is used as proof of a moral or value claim;
- a narrator interprets events for the reader;
- a final report claims the story achieves a theme;
- the user asks for literary depth, thematic closure, or a particular message.

## Compact Theme Surface

For small tasks, record:

- theme or value claim;
- story evidence that supports it;
- counter-pressure or limitation;
- safe wording for the claim;
- status: passed, partial, blocked, stale, human_review, or not_applicable_with_reason.

## Deep Theme Surface

For long-form or final claims, record:

- root theme claim;
- chapter/scene support;
- character arc support;
- promise/payoff support;
- rebuttal or undercutter;
- ending recovery;
- unsafe overclaim wording;
- final safe claim boundary.

## Closure Use

LogicGuard blocks or downgrades closure when the story claims a theme or value
conclusion but the model shows only local scenes, decorative motifs, or broad
summary language without structural support.
