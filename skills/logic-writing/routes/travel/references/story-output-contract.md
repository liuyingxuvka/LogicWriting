# Story Output Contract

The final output should read like a trip, but remain grounded in model evidence. Story output happens after candidate pool, route traces, feasibility checks, fallback links, lodging strategy when needed, fit review, recommendation support, and traveler-native guide compiler surfaces.

## Required Sections

- route options summary;
- why this route is recommended and for whom;
- story route for each main option;
- operational table or list;
- experience candidate notes for important nodes;
- hotel or lodging strategy when applicable;
- risk and pitfall notes;
- fallback options and trigger conditions;
- weather evidence boundary and weather-driven route changes when dates or weather-sensitive nodes matter;
- evidence boundary;
- pre-departure recheck list;
- reverse-guide review status for the delivered artifact, kept in closure or appendix rather than exposed inside the main guide.

## Story Rules

- Use reader-native language.
- Show the day as a lived sequence: entry, build, rest, peak, closure, and exit.
- Compile from a traveler-facing brief, not directly from candidate cards or route trace rows.
- Preserve practical caveats in ordinary language.
- Do not expose internal field names, validation ids, or workflow labels in the traveler-facing story.
- Do not invent sensory detail, pricing, opening hours, wait times, or local facts.
- Do not invent weather, historical daily conditions, forecast details, or weather advisories.
- Do not make an uncertain route feel guaranteed.
- Do not smooth over missing candidate evidence, missing traces, feasibility gaps, hotel constraints, fatigue risk, missing local texture, unpaid experience promises, weak day handoffs, or fallback limits.
- Do not remove all section structure from a multi-day guide. Natural prose still needs readable day or date anchors.

## Traveler-Native Guide Gate

Before delivering final traveler-facing text, confirm:

- travel scene contracts exist for important days and nodes;
- key guide promises are paid, inverted with support, downgraded, deferred with boundary, or human-reviewed;
- multi-day outputs have day interfaces with concrete state handoff;
- multi-day traveler-facing outputs have day/date headings and prose under each day;
- local texture index covers food, shops or souvenirs, rest, negative signals, and named fallbacks at a density matching the trip scope;
- weather evidence status is visible when weather affects route order, route comfort, fallbacks, or claim level;
- reverse-guide review reads the final text and finds no internal label leakage.

## Operational Appendix

The operational appendix should preserve:

- time windows;
- place names and candidate ids in source-facing artifacts;
- transport notes;
- booking or ticket notes;
- hotel and luggage notes;
- walking load;
- price caveats;
- risk triggers;
- fallback choices;
- current-source and recheck status.

The operational appendix may preserve source-facing ids only when it is clearly separate from the traveler-facing guide.
