# Traveler Profile

Build this profile before route construction. Missing material fields become fit-review gaps.

## Core Fields

- `traveler_count`
- `ages`
- `companions`: solo, couple, friends, family, children, elderly parents, business, mixed group
- `stamina`: low, medium, high
- `walking_tolerance_km`
- `stairs_or_hills_tolerance`
- `transit_tolerance`: low, medium, high
- `budget_level`
- `interests`
- `must_see`
- `must_avoid`
- `food_constraints`
- `accessibility_needs`
- `language_constraints`
- `safety_constraints`
- `pace`: relaxed, balanced, intensive
- `style`: comfort, depth, food, photography, history, romantic, child-friendly, elderly-friendly, flexible

## Fit Signals

Use the profile to judge:

- walking load;
- transfer count;
- time pressure;
- rest spacing;
- meal reliability;
- weather exposure;
- queue tolerance;
- safety and accessibility;
- whether the story arc matches the travel purpose.

Do not average a group into one generic traveler. When children, elderly travelers, low stamina, accessibility needs, or food constraints are present, route design must show how those constraints are protected.
