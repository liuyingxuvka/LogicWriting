# City couple native guide

## Day 1 — Arrival afternoon

Start the first afternoon from the central hotel, not from a checklist. After luggage storage, use the main station as the easy anchor, then walk to the design shop street while both travelers still have attention for small stores and souvenir shelves. Keep the first proper meal close: a local bistro dinner, a cafe rest stop, or a market snack works better than chasing a famous restaurant across town when arrival fatigue is still present.

## Day 2 — Full city day

The second day should feel wider but still controlled. The weather evidence for this fixture is a climate-level planning boundary rather than a day-of forecast, so the guide remains an initial plan and requires a forecast and official-alert recheck before booking. If the weather is clear, reserve the evening for the riverside view after an early dinner; if rain or strong wind becomes the trigger, switch to the nearby indoor gallery and keep the hotel buffer as the fallback. This protects the couple pace: food is concrete, shopping is a real browsing segment, rest is planned before the peak, and the risk plan has a nearby replacement rather than a vague warning.

## Day 3 — Departure morning

For the last morning, do not add a far attraction. Use the hotel area, a quiet cafe, one final souvenir shelf, and a short market walk so departure still feels like part of the trip. The main risk is overloading a travel day after dinner and shopping; if fatigue becomes the trigger, return to the hotel, keep the local snack for later, and leave the riverside or gallery as the optional branch.

The sample proves only a far-future planning shape. Opening hours, prices, reservations, transport, forecast details, and alerts must be checked again for the real dates.
