# Near-future bookable city guide

## Day 1 — Arrival and design street

Begin at the central hotel, store luggage, and use the main station as the arrival anchor. The forecast and official advisory fixture are checked for the three-day window. Keep the first design-shop walk short, take a cafe rest if arrival fatigue appears, and move the local bistro dinner earlier if the queue trigger becomes material.

## Day 2 — Food, view, and weather branch

Use the forecast rain window and wind check before leaving dinner. If the checked evening window stays clear and no advisory is active, continue to the riverside viewpoint. If rain, strong wind, or an alert becomes the trigger, switch to the nearby indoor gallery. The gallery is the named fallback, while the hotel-area cafe remains the lower-energy fallback.

## Day 3 — Departure buffer

Keep the final morning near the hotel with one cafe rest and a small souvenir stop. Current-source checks in this frozen scenario support the bookable route shape, but real opening hours, availability, prices, transport, forecast, and alerts must still be refreshed for an actual trip.
