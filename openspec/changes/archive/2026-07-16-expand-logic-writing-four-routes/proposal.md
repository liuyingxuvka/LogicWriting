## Why

Logic Writing currently gives investigation and academic writing one reliable entrypoint, but fiction and deep travel-guide work duplicate the same model-to-reader, actual-artifact, freshness, and closure machinery in separate public skills. Consolidating those related writing systems removes sibling-route duplication, lets each genre improve the others, and gives users one formal writing entrypoint without replacing specialist Guard skills.

## What Changes

- **BREAKING**: expand `logic-writing` from two final-owner routes to four: `investigation`, `academic-writing`, `fiction-writing`, and `travel-guide`.
- **BREAKING**: retire the active `storyline-design` and `travel-story-planner` skill ids after the replacement is installed, globally routed, publicly released, and fresh-clone verified; no aliases, fallback readers, or parallel success routes remain.
- Add a shared reader-native writing kernel for unit contribution, reader-state handoff, model-to-artifact binding, explanation pressure, register ownership, variation pressure, actual-artifact reverse audit, and verifier-derived closure.
- Add a fiction-writing route that preserves short, guarded, and longform depth; story contribution; promises and payoffs; scene/chapter interfaces; continuity; voice; real-manuscript identity; semantic review; and model-prose binding.
- Add a travel-guide route that preserves traveler profile, time-sensitive source modes, weather and alert evidence, candidate and route feasibility, lodging, traveler fit, negative evidence, reachable fallbacks, traveler-native projection, and reverse-guide review.
- Strengthen investigation and academic writing with reader-state movement, question recovery, model-artifact binding, explanation-pressure review, register/variation review, negative evidence, execution friction, and explicit recheck or fallback conditions.
- Add WorldGuard as a preserved specialist owner and replace Travel Story Planner's call to Storyline Design with a neutral shared reader-projection kernel, preventing sibling-route ownership cycles.
- Rebuild FlowGuard ownership, model mesh, Behavior Commitment Ledger, Model-Test Alignment, TestMesh, SkillGuard declared-check inventory, installation projection, global route, release gates, and sequential predecessor privatization evidence.
- Publish the consolidated source as Logic Writing `v2.0.0`, then make `travel-story-planner` private first and `storyline-design-skill` private second, with replacement health checks and rollback gates between the two changes.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `fiction-writing-route`: Model-first short-story, novel, chapter, and final-manuscript planning, drafting, revision, audit, and closure.
- `travel-guide-route`: Evidence-current, feasible, traveler-fit, fallback-aware, story-shaped travel-guide production and closure.
- `predecessor-retirement`: Recoverable installation, public release, fresh-clone validation, active-route removal, and dependency-ordered private visibility for the two predecessor repositories.

### Modified Capabilities

- `unified-routing`: Select exactly one of four final owners and express mixed work only as bounded child requests or shared-kernel consumption.
- `investigation-route`: Add reader-state, model-artifact binding, negative-evidence, fallback/recheck, and genre-native reverse-audit requirements.
- `academic-writing-route`: Add section-level reader-state, question recovery, model-prose binding, explanation-pressure, register, variation, and execution-friction requirements.
- `reader-facing-synthesis`: Generalize the two-room boundary and ReaderBrief into a route-neutral base with route-specific projections and actual-artifact semantic audits.
- `evidence-freshness-closure`: Add route-specific minimum surfaces, real-artifact identity, child-Guard receipt binding, model-mesh closure, and four-route completion rules.
