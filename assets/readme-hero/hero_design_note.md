# Logic Writing README hero design note

- **Project summary:** one Codex skill that routes deep investigation or academic writing through a shared evidence-to-reader pipeline.
- **Target users:** people using Codex for difficult source-backed reports, papers, theses, literature reviews, and substantive revision.
- **Core problem:** research depth, argument structure, and reader-ready prose are often handled as separate or competing workflows.
- **Core workflow:** evidence enters on the left, passes through the `Investigation` or `Academic writing` route while specialist ownership remains bounded, then converges into a `Reader-ready artifact` on the right.
- **Hero tagline:** One entrypoint for deep investigation and academic writing, with evidence boundaries readers can actually follow.
- **Visual concept:** an airy, dimensional evidence loom. Source cards feed two colored lanes through a guarded central hub; the lanes become a clean document rather than an internal dashboard.
- **Image keywords:** evidence cards, two routes, bounded specialists, woven reasoning, clear prose, light technical product render.
- **Files:** `assets/readme-hero/hero.png` and this note. The reproducible vector source is kept only under the ignored `work/` directory.
- **README position:** immediately below the title, public-safe badges, and language link.

The hero is deterministically rasterized because the exact route names and
artifact label are part of the explanation. This avoids unreadable generated
text and keeps the public asset reproducible; it is not a reusable card-grid
template.
